# Decision Memo: Replace The Website With A Guided Proof System

## Decision

The company should replace the current browse-first website model with a guided proof system.

This means the root experience should no longer behave like a traditional portfolio or marketing homepage.
It should behave like a one-screen-at-a-time review flow that helps enterprise reviewers move through proof safely, clearly, and quickly.

## Why This Decision Is Being Made

The current business goal is not to maximize browsing.
The goal is to help a reviewer understand, trust, and act on the strongest proof with minimal confusion.

The current and legacy website model creates several problems:

- too much information appears too early
- users must assemble the story for themselves
- the archive competes with the main message
- accessibility and cognitive simplicity are weakened by normal website behaviors
- premium trust is reduced when the experience feels like content exploration instead of guided review

The guided proof system better matches the company goal because it:

- reduces cognitive load
- makes the strongest proof easier to find
- increases trust through structure and clarity
- supports older adults, disabled users, and low-confidence users
- preserves archive depth without making it the first interaction model

## Product Position

This is not a “homepage redesign.”
This is a product-model change.

We are shifting from:

- browse-first
- website-like
- archive-forward
- marketing-pattern behavior

to:

- guide-first
- service-like
- proof-forward
- procedural and trustworthy behavior

## What The New Experience Should Feel Like

The target feeling is:

- calm
- official
- predictable
- trustworthy
- low-pressure
- completion-oriented

The experience should feel closer to a public-service review or voting-style kiosk than a startup website.

## Expected Gains

### Strategic Gains

- clearer market differentiation
- stronger enterprise trust
- better alignment with the company mission
- better translation of deep technical work into recruiter-safe review flow

### Product Gains

- higher completion rate
- faster time to strongest proof
- lower abandonment from overload

### Business Gains

- more recruiter follow-up

## Main Risks

| Risk | Description | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scope drift | Team slips back into improving the old website instead of replacing it | Critical | Freeze the north star and approve the guided system as the primary product |
| Content overload | Too much proof leaks into the main path | Critical | Enforce main path vs appendix rules |
| Accessibility late work | Accessibility gets treated as cleanup | Critical | Put accessibility lead in the core team from day 1 |
| Copy complexity | Dense language undermines the entire experience | Critical | Write plain-language copy before major build work |
| Engineering instability | Legacy repo and build behavior slow execution | High | Stabilize the codebase and isolate the new wizard path |
| Validation gap | Team assumes simplicity without testing real users | Critical | Test with older adults, disabled users, and low-literacy users early |
| Stakeholder sprawl | Too many opinions add extra steps and options | High | Product owner has final say on main-path scope |

## Team View

Overall team sentiment is positive.
The team believes the guided proof system is the correct direction.

However, the team also sees that success depends on discipline.
If the company tries to keep both a normal website and a guided system equally primary, the result will likely become confusing and expensive.

## Highest ROI Staffing Recommendation

The highest ROI move is to activate a focused guided-experience team rather than a broad general team.

### Core Team To Activate Now

- Product Owner
- Frontend Lead
- Accessibility Lead
- UX Content Strategist
- QA / Accessibility Test Engineer

### Why This Team Is Highest ROI

- Product Owner protects scope
- Frontend Lead makes the system real
- Accessibility Lead protects the target users
- UX Content Strategist removes confusion at the source
- QA protects trust and release quality

## Highest ROI Execution Sequence

1. Lock the final wizard screen map.
2. Write the full plain-language copy deck.
3. Normalize the archive into proof packets.
4. Build the wizard shell and state model.
5. Add save/resume and progress.
6. Validate with real target users.
7. Release only after comprehension and accessibility pass.

## What Should Happen Right Now

### Immediate Company Decision

Approve the guided proof system as the primary product direction.

### Immediate Team Decision

Stand up the guided-experience team and give the Product Owner final say over the main path.

### Immediate Delivery Decision

Do not continue with “website enhancement” work as the default path.
Treat the overhaul as a controlled product replacement.

## Success Criteria

The decision is successful when:

- the root route is a guided proof flow
- each screen is understandable in seconds
- the user always knows the next action
- archive exploration is optional, not required
- completion and follow-up improve
- accessibility is validated with real users

## Recommendation

Proceed with the guided proof system.

This is the highest-confidence path to a simpler, more accessible, more premium enterprise review experience.
The company should commit to this direction fully rather than trying to preserve the old website model as an equal primary experience.
